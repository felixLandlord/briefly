## 🏢 Company Overview

Really Great Tech is a boutique software development consultancy with operations spanning Tel Aviv, Israel and Accra, Ghana [1]. Founded by a leadership team with 15+ years of combined experience, RGT positions itself as "Pioneers in AI, specializing in evolution" [1]. The company serves a broad client base of 500+ "innovators" across global markets, though its small workforce of 11–50 employees suggests a services model reliant on lean, high-utilization teams [1].

RGT's dual-geo structure is central to its value proposition: Israeli technical leadership combined with Ghanaian talent for cost arbitrage. This hybrid model allows them to offer competitive pricing while maintaining proximity to Western markets and compliance regimes [1].

## 🔴 Weaknesses & Gaps

2. **No Disclosed Funding** — Bootstrapped or self-funded means they lack war chest for aggressive hiring, marketing, or R&D compared to VC-backed competitors.
3. **Geographic Brand Risk** — Ghana-to-US delivery raises questions for some US buyers about timezone alignment, cultural fit, and communication (though Israeli HQ partially mitigates this).
4. **Fixed Price = Margin Compression** — If Medical AI projects encounter unexpected complexity, their fixed-price model bleeds margin. 8-week HIPAA delivery is aggressive — compliance gaps or scope creep are real risks.
5. **Vague Differentiation in Generic Services** — "Custom Web & Mobile" is table stakes. Without deep vertical specialization beyond healthcare, they compete on generic criteria with hundreds of shops globally.
6. **Blockchain Services Stagnation** — Blockchain hype has faded; if this is a core offering, it's likely a loss-leader or low-margin commodity service.

## 🎯 Competitive Implications

**For your team selling against RGT:**

- **Attack their capacity ceiling** — If you're a larger firm with 100+ engineers, emphasize your ability to scale without the bottlenecks a 50-person shop faces on enterprise-sized engagements.
- **Challenge delivery guarantees** — An 8-week HIPAA-compliant Medical AI deployment is aggressive. Probe for case studies, audit reports, and client references. Any slip reveals the fixed-price model as marketing, not reality.
- **Leverage geographic bias** — Some US enterprises prefer US-only or US-heavy delivery teams. If your firm has onshore dominance, lean into compliance peace-of-mind.

**For your team selling alongside or to RGT partners:**

- **Position as complementary expertise** — If you serve the same ICP (startups, healthcare ISVs), consider white-label or referral arrangements. Their lean team may need specialized sub-service expertise you provide.
- **Target their healthcare vertical** — They open doors with HIPAA compliance; you can provide the specialized ML ops, data engineering, or regulatory consulting they're too small to own end-to-end.

**Strategic threat level**: ⭐⭐⭐ (Moderate) — RGT is not a market leader, but their HIPAA + fixed-price positioning carves out a defensible niche that could attract buyers frustrated with T&M uncertainty from larger consultancies.
